religion

The Rwandan government has shut down thousands of churches and mosques for failing to meet safety, hygiene, and noise regulations. The Rwanda Governance Board (RGB) cited issues like poor infrastructure, lack of sanitation, and unqualified leaders. Since 2018, over 8,000 religious institutions have been closed, with authorities insisting that compliance is necessary for public safety. While some see this as a crackdown on religious freedom, the government argues it promotes responsible worship. Religious groups can reopen if they meet the requirements, but the move has sparked debate over the balance between regulation and faith in Rwanda.

Rwanda Marks 31st National Heroes' Day with Tribute and Unity Kigali, February 1, 2025 Rwanda celebrated its 31st National Heroes' Day, honoring those who made immense sacrifices for the nation's freedom and development.
